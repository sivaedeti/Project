Beaches

The Goa that we hear about – sizzling nightlife, hippie markets, and the glitz & glamor – is, in fact, North Goa. The more explored and frequently-visited coastline of Goa is the stretch to the North of Fort Aguada up to the borders of Maharashtra.

BAGA BEACH

Baga beach which is situated in North Goa is one of the favorite hangouts for
nature enthusiasts, where one can relish the scenic beauty of a place.
Watersports are the major attraction of Baga Beach.One can also go for
dolphin spotting tours and island trips from the beach. It has also got
the best nightlife scenes in Goa.The best time to visit Baga Beach is between
October and March when the weather is pleasant and parties are held almost
every night at the beach.

Architectural style: Baroque architecture
Opened: 1605
Height: 18 m

CALANGUTE BEACH

Among the most popular beaches for British tourists spending their holidays
in Goa,Calangute’s long strips of golden sand result in one of the most busie
st beaches in Goa. Encircled by palm and coconut trees, the beach is spread f
our miles along the Arabian Sea and nine miles from the Panaji.So, if you want
an unstrained solitude, plan for Calangute beach holidays as it's a perfect
destination for beach bums.
SINQUERIM BEACH
Sinquerim Beach boasts an amazing shoreline and it is located north of the all popular
beaches of Goa. Sinquerim is popular among tourists flocking
from all over the world. One of the key attractions of Sinquerim Beach
is the popular Sunburn Festival held each year in December. It is home
to many restaurants and shacks that serve fresh seafood and cocktails
to pamper your taste buds.
ANJUNA BEACH
This is the oldest and most famous mosques in Goa which was built in 1560 by
Ibrahim Adilshan of Bijapur. The star attraction of Safa Masjid is the huge laterite
stone masonry tank with turquoise waters located within the mosque complex.
Many Mosques were destroyed during the Portuguese rule but this Safa Masjid
remained unharmed.According to V.T. Gune, the mosque was built in 1560 by the
Bijapuri ruler Ibrahim Adil Shah I about 2 km from center of Ponda.The mosque
may be datable to the Adil shah period or even earlier.

VAGATOR BEACH
Located in North Goa Vagator beach is a curved stretch of sand overlooked by the
Chapora Fort. The beach is dotted with a hillock on one side & headland on the other.
The beach itself is divided into two parts, Big Vagator & Little Vagator. It is known
as the most photographed beach in Goa. November to March is the best time to
visit Vagator Beach to have the most fun.
